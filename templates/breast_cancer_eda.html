4. Distance_km Dimension Plot

Observation:

The green dots indicate that for most distances, the delivery time is low, meaning deliveries are generally on time regardless of the distance traveled. The orange dots represent medium delivery times, suggesting that as distance increases, there are some cases where delivery takes a bit longer but still remains within a reasonable range. The sparse red dots show that late deliveries are rare, even for longer distances.
Interpretation:
Distance doesn't seem to be a major factor in causing delivery delays in this dataset. This could imply that the logistics system is well-optimized for handling varying distances, possibly due to efficient routing, reliable transportation, or experienced couriers who manage longer trips effectively. It's a positive sign for delivering essential goods, as timely delivery is maintained across distances.

5. Courier_Experience_yrs Dimension Plot

Observation:

Analysis:
The prevalence of green dots along this axis suggests that couriers, regardless of their years of experience, are mostly able to deliver on time.
