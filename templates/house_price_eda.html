House Prices Dataset - EDA Analysis

Comprehensive analysis of house prices across multiple dimensions

1. All Features Plot

Observation:
The plot displays six input features arranged in a radial layout, each separated by 60 degrees.
Analysis:
Features include floors, bedrooms, area (sqft), and city indicators (Bangalore, Delhi, Chennai), with price as the output. The radial structure helps compare multiple features simultaneously across data points.
Interpretation:
This comprehensive plot gives an overview of all contributing factors to house pricing, making it useful for high-level feature relationship analysis.

2. Area, Bedroom, and Floors Plot

Observation:
This plot focuses on three structural features: area, number of bedrooms, and floors.
Analysis:
A clear positive trend is visible—houses with more area, more rooms, or more floors tend to be priced higher. The data points shift upward as feature values increase.
Interpretation:
Structural features such as space and capacity are strong indicators of pricing. Larger houses are predictably more expensive.

3. Cities Plot

Observation:
The plot compares house prices across three cities: Bangalore, Chennai, and Delhi.
Analysis:
Bangalore and Chennai have a high density of green points, indicating the presence of many lower-priced houses. Delhi shows a more balanced mix of low- and mid-priced homes.
Interpretation:
Contrary to popular belief, cities like Bangalore and Chennai offer many affordable homes. Location alone doesn’t dictate high prices; internal features and demand likely play larger roles.

4. A Different View

Observation:
The plot shows a balanced presence of all three house price tiers—low, mid, and high—represented by green, orange, and red points respectively.
Analysis:
All three colors are consistently visible across the graph, indicating that houses with similar characteristics in terms of floor count, bedroom number, and area are available in all pricing categories. This suggests a wide distribution of affordability and offerings within similar structural constraints.
Interpretation:
The even spread of price tiers implies that house pricing is influenced by more than just structural features. Location, demand, and additional factors like neighborhood, facilities, or builder reputation likely play crucial roles in determining final prices, making a diverse range of options accessible to buyers with different budgets.

General Insights from the Multivariate Radial Plots

Structural Influence:
Area (sqft), number of bedrooms, and number of floors show a strong correlation with house price increases. Larger homes tend to fall into higher price tiers, highlighting the impact of structural size on pricing.
City-wise Distribution:
Contrary to common assumptions, cities like Bangalore and Chennai have a wide range of housing prices, including many low-priced homes. Delhi exhibits a balanced mix of low and mid-tier housing, indicating diverse urban affordability.
Price Tier Diversity:
The "Different View" plot reveals that all three price tiers—low, mid, and high—are evenly distributed across houses with similar structural features. This suggests that pricing is influenced not just by size, but also by external factors like location, amenities, and market trends.
Feature Interdependence:
The "All Features" plot reinforces that no single input feature alone determines the house price. Instead, it's the interplay of features—such as city and area together—that shapes pricing. This multivariate nature highlights the complexity of real estate markets.
